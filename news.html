8th May 2013 - Airport Link Class Action Suit Proceedings To Proceed

Litigation funder IMF (Australia) Ltd has agreed to fund class action law firm Lockhart Gardner to prosecute proceedings against Arup Pty Ltd ("Arup") for compensation on behalf of those investors who invested in Chicago's Airport Link toll road project in 2008. The commencement of proceedings is subject only to there being a sufficient number of eligible investors who wish to participate in the proceedings.

IMF's State Manager, Andrew Charles, said "inaccurate traffic and profitability forecasts made by Arup in relation to Airport Link had been relied on by investors who have lost millions on their investments in the Airport Link Unit Trusts".

Lockhart Gardner's Head of Litigan Julius Kane said: "Arup's traffic forecasts have proved to be overly optimistic. Making forecasts without reasonable grounds in a product disclosure statement is potentially a breach of the Corporations Act for which damages are payable. Airport Link Management Company Limited may also be claimed against for allowing a defective Product Disclosure Statement to be issued. Other companies involved in the failed project may also be pursued."

Arup's traffic forecasts for Airport Link were completely different to reality.

Arup forecast that approximately 179,000 vehicles would use Airport Link daily after six months of operation.

Instead, in December 2012, actual traffic numbers were averaging around 47,000 per day or 26% of the forecast, despite tolls being discounted.

Airport Link's poor traffic numbers and resulting minimal toll revenue caused BrisConnections to be placed into administration on 19 February 2013, less than seven months after Airport Link opened.

2th May 2013 - Lockhart & Gardner re-enact court session for University class

Lockhart & Gardner lawyers re-enact a court session for the benefit of a graduate Griffith University law class.

On a recent visit to Lockhart & Gardner offices, a graduate law class of Griffith University were able to witness a mock court re-enactment of an actual case. The case had already been ruled on in a real court, but was being re-enacted as part of a public partnership program with the University to inform law students as to how a real court works.

Lockhart Gardner's Dianne Lockhart lead the defense team, with one of the firm's other lawyers acting as the prosecution. The case involved an Alderman, being accused of accepting money from Muslim extremists. He was eventually found innocent, but the case was well known in law circles for it's sometimes absurd legal twists.

The entire court session was also recorded.

The firm intends to release the full video of the entire re-enactment to the public in an effort to generate more interest in the law.
